The Core Difference

Google ranking and ChatGPT mentions are different jobs.

Good search performance helps your pages get found. It does not guarantee that the model will lift your business into the answer. The model still needs clean evidence it can trust and quote.

Google

Ranks pages worth showing

Search engines decide which pages belong in results. That is a visibility problem at the page level.

  • Shows pages in response to a query
  • Rewards relevance and search intent
  • Helps discovery, not final phrasing
ChatGPT

Reuses facts it can trust

Answer engines decide which pieces of information are clear, credible, and relevant enough to reuse inside the final response.

  • Looks for patterns, not just pages
  • Needs stronger category clarity
  • Favours evidence it can quote cleanly
What AI Needs

Five signals make a business easier to mention.

From the Domino material and the blog itself, the pattern is consistent: AI systems favour businesses that are easy to categorise, easy to verify, and easy to quote.

1
Clear company description Say what the business is in direct language, not just tone and aspiration.
2
Answer-first structure Use clear headings, clear answers, and buyer-shaped language the model can lift.
3
Consistent facts Keep the same core story across the site, bios, mentions, and public references.
4
Supporting evidence Use specific service descriptions, proof points, labels, and direct definitions.
5
Reusable chunks Two or three clean sentences can outperform a long stretch of vague copy.
Why Competitors Win

The clearer brand often gets the mention.

When owners ask why a competitor gets named instead, the instinct is to blame rankings. Sometimes rankings matter. Very often the real gap is clarity and consistency.

Blurry Brand

Harder for AI to trust

The business may still rank, but the model has to guess what it is, how it should be described, and whether the signals really line up.

  • Mixed labels across pages
  • Benefits explained before the category
  • Important facts scattered across the site
  • Thin support outside the main domain
Clear Brand

Easier for AI to reuse

The brand looks more certain because the model sees the same core story repeated cleanly, with enough evidence to support the choice.

  • Direct category language
  • Strong FAQ and answer-first sections
  • Consistent wording across the site
  • Better corroboration beyond the website
Q
Mentioned is not enough

The same research picture behind the article also notes that 97% of consumers read reviews for local businesses, while 88% fact-check reviews cited by AI. Clear and credible beats vague and partially correct.

How To Tell

Check the answer, not just the analytics.

A lot of businesses only spot the issue when they test real prompts and see what comes back. The practical check is simple: run buyer-style prompts, compare the outputs, and inspect the signal quality.

Prompt Audit
Who are the best [category] providers in [location]?

Checks whether the brand appears in commercial shortlists.

Which companies help with [problem you solve]?

Tests category clarity and commercial positioning.

What does [your brand] do?

Shows whether the model can describe the business cleanly.

What are the best alternatives to [competitor]?

Reveals whether the brand gets picked up in lateral comparisons.

What to look for
  • Are you mentioned at all?
  • Is the description accurate?
  • Does the category sound right?
  • Do competitors appear more often than you?
  • Are strong facts scattered instead of clearly stated?
  • Does the wider footprint look thin or inconsistent?
What To Fix First

Do not publish more noise. Improve the shape of the information.

The first job is to make your most important information easier to understand and easier to trust. That means better structure, better wording, and a measurable baseline before random activity begins.

Start Here

Tighten the core description

Make sure the main pages say what the business is, who it helps, and what problem it solves in direct language.

  • Repeat the same wording where it matters most
  • Lead with the category, not only the benefits
Page Shape

Rework key pages into answer-first pages

Broad marketing copy is weak material for AI systems. Strong FAQs, definitions, and comparison blocks are easier to reuse.

  • See the Domino FAQ page
  • Read the related article
Support Signals

Improve the signal beyond your website

You do not need to be everywhere. You do need enough supporting evidence that the business feels real, specific, and consistent across the web.

  • Use founder profiles, public references, and credible mentions
  • Keep the same core message everywhere

Why this matters

If your brand misses the shortlist, it misses the decision moment.

The question is not whether your business has a website. The question is whether AI systems can understand it well enough to trust, describe, and reuse it.

Category clarity Reusable answers Consistent facts
Why doesn't ChatGPT mention my business even though I rank on Google?

Because ranking a page and selecting a brand for an AI answer are different decisions.

  • Google decides which pages to show.
  • ChatGPT decides which facts it can reuse.
  • A page can rank and still be too vague for the model.
How do I get my business to show up in ChatGPT answers?

Make the business easier to understand, verify, and quote.

  • Use direct category language.
  • Rework key pages into answer-first pages.
  • Strengthen consistent signals across public sources.
Why does ChatGPT recommend my competitor instead of me?

The competitor often looks clearer and more certain, not just better ranked.

  • Stronger FAQ coverage helps.
  • More consistent wording helps.
  • Better corroboration helps.
Does ChatGPT only use my website to understand my business?

No. The website matters, but it is not always enough on its own.

  • Profiles and bios can reinforce the category.
  • Mentions and reviews help confirm facts.
  • Thin outside signals can still weaken the picture.
What makes content easier for ChatGPT to mention?

Content becomes easier to mention when it answers real buyer questions directly.

  • Clear headings help.
  • Useful definitions help.
  • Short reusable chunks help.
How do I check what ChatGPT currently says about my business?

Run buyer-style prompts and inspect the output critically.

  • Check whether the brand appears.
  • Check whether the category is correct.
  • Check which signals seem to shape the answer.
Do I need to publish more content to get mentioned in ChatGPT?

Not usually. The first problem is often the shape of the information.

  • Generic content does not fix weak structure.
  • Clearer information matters more than more information.
  • Supporting evidence still needs the same message.
What should I fix first if ChatGPT is ignoring my business?

Start with the core company description and the key pages.

  • State what the business is clearly.
  • Repeat that wording consistently.
  • Baseline AI outputs before wider changes.
